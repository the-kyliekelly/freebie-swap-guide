The Setup Checklist

Get these six things in place before you pitch anyone. They make the swap easy for your partner to say yes to, and easy for you to execute.

01
Crystal-clear ideal client
You need to be able to recognise an aligned partner immediately. If you're fuzzy on who you serve, you'll pitch the wrong people and waste both your time.
02
A high-converting freebie with a strong title
The title needs to name the outcome, not the format. "5-Day Email Challenge" is a format. "Write a Week of Emails in 90 Minutes" is an outcome. Test it on your own list first. If your people don't opt in, theirs won't either.
03
A landing page with an opt-in form
Connected to your email list and ready to receive new subscribers. This is the link your partner will share. Make sure it loads quickly and the form works.
04
A mockup graphic of your freebie
Your partner needs something visual to include in their email. A clean mockup makes your freebie look polished and worth grabbing. Canva works perfectly for this.
05
A welcome email ready to send
New subscribers from the swap need a warm introduction to you. Your welcome email is the first impression. Make it personal, deliver the freebie, and give them a reason to keep reading.
06
A swipe copy doc and tagging ready in your ESP
Prepare a Google Doc with your subject line options, email body and all assets. Tag incoming subscribers by source so you can track results. Making it effortless for your partner to say yes is the whole game.
Reminder

A freebie that converts beats a freebie that looks pretty. Get the conversion rate up on your own list before you take it to someone else's audience.

The Freebie Swap Process

Take it one step at a time. The simpler you keep it, the more swaps you'll actually complete.

FIND
Step 1: Identify an aligned partner
Ask: would my ideal client be in their audience? They can be complementary (different skill, same client) or even a direct competitor. Start with your existing network. It's easier to pitch someone who already knows you exist.
CONNECT
Step 2: Build the relationship first
No one likes a cold pitch. Engage with their content genuinely. Comment on their emails. Share their podcast. Listen before you ask. The pitch lands so much better when it doesn't come out of nowhere.
PITCH
Step 3: Send a personalised pitch
DM first (a voice note on Instagram works brilliantly), then follow up by email. Keep it short, specific and warm. You're not asking for a favour. You're offering mutual value. The pitch comes after the relationship.
PREPARE
Step 4: Write swipe copy and set a date
Once you have a yes, set a date within two weeks while the energy is fresh. Write the swipe copy (see the Swipe Copy tab for exactly what to include) and send it with your landing page link and mockup image.
SUBSCRIBE
Step 5: Sign up to their list yourself
Do this so you can see their email land in real time. You'll know it went out, you can see how they positioned it, and it holds the whole thing accountable. Plus it's just good form.
SEND
Step 6: Send your email to your list
You send their freebie to your list on the same agreed date (or within the same window). Make it genuine, warm and personal. Your list trusts you. Use that trust well.
REPORT
Step 7: Share results and say thank you
Message your partner with the numbers. How many clicks, how many opt-ins. It closes the loop, builds the relationship, and opens the door to doing it again. A warm thank you goes a long way.

What Makes a Great Pitch

The mindset shift: you're not asking for a favour. You're offering mutual value. This is a collaboration, not a transaction.

01 Personalise
Use their name
Reference something specific about their work. Show you actually pay attention.
02 Frame the value
What's in it for them?
Explain what you bring and why it benefits their audience specifically.
03 Keep it short
Be clear and direct
End with a direct call to action. One ask, not three questions.
04 How to send
DM first, email second
A voice note on Instagram works brilliantly. Follow up by email if no response.
Follow-up protocol

If you hear nothing, wait 5 to 7 days then send one warm follow-up. Keep it short: "Hey [name], just bumping this in case it got lost!" If still no reply, let it go. No chasing. A non-response is not a no. It's just not a yes yet. Come back in a few months.

What to say

Pitch template structure

Pitch DM or email structure

Hey [Name],


I've been following your work for a while and [specific thing you love about what they do]. I think our audiences have a lot of overlap.


I'd love to do a freebie swap with you. I share your [freebie name] with my list of [size] and you share mine with yours. No cost, no complicated logistics, just a simple email each.


My freebie is [name and one-line description]. Here's the landing page: [link]


Would you be open to it?


[Your name]

💡
Mark your calendar. Set a reminder for 7 to 14 days after pitching so you don't forget to follow up. Most people aren't ignoring you. They're just busy.
💡
Move on quickly. While you're waiting for one person to reply, pitch the next. Keep the pipeline warm so no single yes or no makes or breaks your momentum.
Choosing wisely

Green Flags vs. Red Flags

Who you swap with matters more than how many times you swap. One aligned partner is worth ten misaligned ones.

Green flags
Serves your exact ideal client
Complementary or different angle to yours
Has an engaged list (not just a big one)
Active and responsive online
Their freebie is different from yours
Has a landing page and a welcome sequence
Red flags
Just a friend with no audience overlap
Same freebie topic as yours
Inactive or inconsistent online presence
Never engages with their own email list
No landing page or welcome sequence in place
Ghost their subscribers after the freebie
The key question

Ask yourself: would my ideal client be in their audience? If yes, it's worth exploring. If you're not sure, look at who engages with their content and what problems they solve.

Where to find aligned partners

  • Your existing network first. It's always easier to pitch someone who already knows you. Check who's in your communities, your DMs, your inbox.
  • Podcasts in your space. Who's being interviewed? Who's interviewing others? Those people have built audiences that trust them.
  • Communities you're already in. Skool groups, Facebook groups, Slack channels. Look for who's actively sharing value.
  • People you're already subscribed to. If you're on their list, you already know their content. That makes the pitch genuine.
  • Collaborative bundles. Who else contributed? These are pre-qualified aligned peers.
Abundant mindset

A rising tide lifts all ships. Your "competitors" can absolutely be your swap partners. Shared audiences often have room for multiple trusted voices, especially if you each bring a different angle.

Swipe Copy, Timing and Cadence

The easier you make it for your partner, the more likely they are to actually send it. Great swipe copy does the heavy lifting for them.

What goes in your swipe copy doc

  • 3 subject line options. Give them variety. Some audiences respond to curiosity, others to direct benefit. More options means a better match to their voice.
  • A full email body they can copy-paste. Written in their voice as much as possible. They should feel able to use it as-is or tweak lightly.
  • Your landing page link. Triple-check this is working before you send the doc.
  • Your mockup image. A clean, simple visual of your freebie. Include it as a direct link or attachment.
  • A link to your headshot. Optional but useful. Some partners like to add a photo.

What great swipe copy looks like

Subject line options (choose one)
How [Name] attracted 1,100+ ideal clients without social media
Want more subscribers without the hustle? This resource spills it all
Ready to grow your list without burning out?
Email body structure

Hey [First Name],


[HOOK: Start with the problem or desire your freebie solves]
If growing your email list feels harder than it should be, you are not alone.


[RESOURCE: Name it and describe it in one sentence]
I'm excited to share a free resource from my friend [Your Name] that shows a different, better way to build your audience without depending on constant content, reels or DMs.


[SOCIAL PROOF OR SPECIFICITY: Make it tangible]
She shares the exact case study that helped her attract over 1,100 ideal clients without a huge audience, expensive ads, and (best part?) without living online 24/7.


[CTA: One link, one action, no confusion]
[BUTTON: Grab the free resource here]


Packed with real talk that'll leave you feeling more confident about growing your list sustainably.


[Sign off]

Length rule

Keep swipe copy to 150 to 200 words max. Your partner's audience doesn't know you yet. Short, punchy and benefit-led wins every time over long and comprehensive.

How often to swap

  • Start with just one. Get the whole process down before you try to stack multiple swaps at once.
  • Once comfortable: aim for 2 to 3 per month with different partners. This gives you a steady stream of new subscribers without overwhelming your list.
  • Don't stack too many. Your list will notice if every email is promoting someone else's freebie. Space them out.
  • Mix swaps with your own content. Your list stays warm and trusts you when they hear from you, not just about others.
Tagging tip

Set up a tag in your ESP for each swap partner so you can see exactly how many subscribers came from each swap. This data makes your next pitch stronger: "My last swap brought in 47 new subscribers."

The Debrief and Common Mistakes

You can't improve what you don't measure. A quick debrief after each swap tells you exactly what to tweak next time.

What to measure

📬
Open rate
Was your subject line compelling enough for their specific audience?
🖱️
Click rate
Did the swipe copy and mockup do the job of making people want it?
📈
New subscribers
How many people opted in from their send? Tag by source to track this easily.
🎯
What "good" looks like
Even 20 to 30 new aligned subscribers is a genuine win. Quality over volume.
Use the data

After each swap, decide: repeat with this partner, tweak the copy, or try someone new? The numbers tell you everything you need to know.

Common mistakes to avoid

👯
Swapping with friends instead of aligned partners
🧊
Cold pitching before building any relationship
📄
Forgetting to write swipe copy for your partner
🔇
Never following up or saying thank you after
📊
Not tracking results, so nothing improves
🌊
Stacking too many swaps and burning out your list

Your Next Steps

The strategy is simple. The hardest part is starting. Here's everything to check before you go and what to do right now.

What if your freebie isn't ready yet?

  • Don't pitch until your freebie has a clear, specific promise in the title. Outcome over format, always.
  • Test on your own audience first. If your people aren't opting in, a partner's audience won't either.
  • Sharpen the title. Name what someone walks away with, not what the thing is called.
  • Once it's converting well, then reach out. You'll pitch with more confidence too, and confidence shows up in the copy.
